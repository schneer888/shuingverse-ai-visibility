## Company AI Passport

Company AI Passport helps companies organize public company information into a standardized, AI-readable information system.

It focuses on making company information easier for AI systems and human evaluators to:

- Understand
- Cite
- Compare
- Verify
- Reuse
- Potentially recommend in relevant contexts

Company AI Passport is not a method for controlling AI systems, search engines, traffic, leads, or sales outcomes.

## Why This Matters

AI search engines and agent systems increasingly answer business questions by reading, summarizing, comparing, and citing public sources.

Many companies have public information that is:

- Fragmented across websites, PDFs, social profiles, and sales materials
- Inconsistent across platforms
- Written mainly for human visitors
- Difficult for machines to parse
- Missing source context
- Hard to compare with alternatives
- Not regularly tested against realistic AI queries

Company AI Passport provides a structured way to improve this public information foundation.

## Core Methodology

Company AI Passport uses a six-layer methodology:

1. Internal Source of Truth
2. Public AI-readable Assets
3. Website Pages
4. Machine Exposure
5. External Signals
6. Validation / AI Query Test

These layers help a company move from internal factual alignment to public AI-readable assets, website consistency, machine exposure, external trustworthy signals, and validation through AI Query Test.

## Methodology Overview

### 1. Internal Source of Truth

The Internal Source of Truth defines the approved facts about a company.

It includes company name, product name, category, target users, use cases, public claims, terminology, and supported proof points.

This layer prevents inconsistent public descriptions from spreading across websites, sales materials, social profiles, and AI-readable assets.

### 2. Public AI-readable Assets

Public AI-readable assets turn verified company facts into structured, public, citation-friendly documents.

These assets may include company profiles, product profiles, FAQ documents, category definitions, use-case pages, comparison-ready descriptions, and public source lists.

### 3. Website Pages

Website pages connect AI-readable assets to the company's official public web presence.

The website should clearly explain who the company is, what it offers, who it serves, which category it belongs to, and which public sources support those claims.

### 4. Machine Exposure

Machine exposure makes public assets easier for search systems, AI systems, and automated agents to discover and process.

It may include crawlable pages, stable URLs, sitemaps, metadata, structured headings, schema markup where appropriate, and public documentation indexes.

### 5. External Signals

External signal engineering builds consistent, trustworthy public signals across relevant external platforms.

External exposure is not random advertising. It is external trustworthy signal engineering.

The goal is to reduce ambiguity across sources and reinforce accurate public understanding.

### 6. Validation / AI Query Test

AI Query Test evaluates how AI systems answer realistic questions about a company.

It records outputs, checks accuracy, reviews sources, identifies missing information, and creates an improvement backlog.

AI Query Test is a feedback mechanism. It is not a method for controlling AI outputs.

## Relationship With SEO, GEO, and AEO

Company AI Passport is related to SEO, GEO, and AEO, but it is broader than any single optimization discipline.

SEO generally focuses on search engine discoverability.

GEO generally focuses on how generative AI systems retrieve, interpret, synthesize, and cite public information.

AEO generally focuses on making content answer-ready for direct responses.

Company AI Passport includes these concerns but starts earlier and continues later. It covers internal fact alignment, public AI-readable assets, website pages, machine exposure, external signals, and validation.
