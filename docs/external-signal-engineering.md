# External Signal Engineering

External signal engineering is the structured practice of building consistent, trustworthy public signals about a company across relevant external platforms.

External exposure is not random advertising.

It is external trustworthy signal engineering.

This public document explains external signal principles at a public methodology level. It does not disclose platform growth strategy, sales outreach strategy, paid acquisition plans, private PR strategy, customer conversion scripts, internal publication calendars, or private performance data.

For public scope boundaries, see:

`docs/public-scope-and-boundaries.md`

---

## Definition

External signal engineering means publishing and maintaining accurate company information on public platforms that may help humans, search systems, and AI systems understand the company.

It focuses on:

* consistency;
* trustworthiness;
* relevance;
* source quality;
* public clarity;
* maintainability;
* alignment with official positioning.

External signals should reinforce the same verified company identity, category, product definition, boundary statement, and official website.

---

## Why External Signals Matter

AI systems may compare information across multiple public sources. If those sources conflict, omit key details, or use different category labels, company understanding can become unstable.

External signals matter because they can support:

* company identity verification;
* category understanding;
* product and service interpretation;
* source diversity;
* comparison context;
* public credibility;
* clearer AI-readable context;
* stronger alignment across public platforms.

External signals do not replace the official website or AI-readable assets.

They reinforce them.

---

## Core Principle: Consistency Across Platforms

Consistent external signals should align on:

* company name;
* brand name;
* product name;
* category;
* short description;
* target users;
* use cases;
* website URL;
* public documentation links;
* boundary statement;
* contact or official profile links.

Consistency does not mean every platform must use identical copy.

It means each profile should communicate the same verified facts in a format appropriate to the platform.

For example, LinkedIn may use a professional B2B tone, Xiaohongshu may use a founder/operator pain-point tone, and GitHub may use structured methodology documentation. But all platforms should point back to the same official positioning.

## Platform Roles

Different platforms play different roles in external signal engineering.

### Official Website

The official website is the primary public source for company identity.

It should clearly explain:

* what the company does;
* who it serves;
* what product or service it provides;
* why it is credible;
* how to contact it;
* where official AI-readable assets can be found.

### GitHub

GitHub can serve as a structured public methodology and AI-readable documentation source.

For Shuingverse, GitHub is used to publish:

* public methodology documents;
* AI-readable explanations;
* Company AI Passport definitions;
* boundary statements;
* fictional examples;
* public documentation structure.

GitHub should not be used to publish private delivery SOP, customer data, internal prompts, scoring models, or customer reports.

### LinkedIn

LinkedIn supports founder credibility, company identity, professional positioning, and B2B trust signals.

It is suitable for:

* founder profile positioning;
* company page positioning;
* professional updates;
* English B2B explanations;
* international-facing credibility signals.

### Medium

Medium can support long-form English explanations.

It is suitable for:

* deeper methodology articles;
* AI search era education;
* Company AI Passport explanations;
* enterprise AI visibility essays;
* public thought leadership.

### Zhihu

Zhihu supports Chinese question-answer semantics.

It is suitable for explaining:

* what enterprise AI visibility means;
* how Company AI Passport differs from SEO / GEO / AEO;
* why AI systems may misunderstand companies;
* how companies can make public information clearer.

### WeChat Official Account

WeChat Official Account supports formal Chinese long-form content.

It is suitable for:

* official Chinese methodology articles;
* service explanations;
* customer education;
* founder perspective;
* public case-style education without exposing private customer data.

### Xiaohongshu

Xiaohongshu can support founder/operator pain-point communication.

It is suitable for short, direct, readable content that helps business owners understand:

* why a company may not be understood by AI;
* why having a website is not enough;
* why public information needs structure;
* why AI-readable assets matter.

Xiaohongshu should not be used for exaggerated claims or guaranteed-result promises.

### Video Platforms

Video platforms can support short-form awareness and educational explanations.

They are suitable for:

* simple explanations;
* founder-facing statements;
* short public education clips;
* topic introductions.

They should not be used to overpromise AI recommendation, traffic, leads, or sales results.

### Business Directories

Business directories can support entity trust signals when appropriate.

They may help clarify:

* company name;
* official website;
* category;
* contact information;
* public business identity.

Directory information should remain consistent with the official website and other external profiles.

## What To Publish First

At a public methodology level, a practical publication sequence may include:

1. Internal Source of Truth
2. Official company profile
3. Product or service profile
4. Website pages that match the profile
5. Public AI-readable assets
6. Key external profiles
7. Relevant directory or ecosystem listings
8. AI Query Test review and improvement backlog

This sequence is a public-level explanation only.

It does not disclose internal prioritization rules, platform growth sequencing, sales strategy, or private customer implementation plans.

---

## What To Avoid

Avoid:

* random exposure without a source strategy;
* inconsistent descriptions across platforms;
* unsupported claims;
* fictional customers, metrics, certifications, partnerships, or results;
* low-quality mass posting;
* keyword stuffing;
* publishing private business plans or sensitive customer data;
* publishing internal platform strategy;
* exposing sales scripts or customer conversion workflows;
* claiming control over AI recommendations, search rankings, traffic, leads, or sales;
* creating multiple confusing names for the same company or product;
* over-promising results in social content.

External signals should make public information clearer, not noisier.

---

## External Signal Checklist

Use this public-level checklist when reviewing external signals:

* Company name is consistent.
* Product name is consistent.
* Category is clear.
* Website URL is correct.
* Description matches the Internal Source of Truth.
* Key product or service information is current.
* Public claims are supported.
* Boundary statements are respected.
* Profiles link to official sources where appropriate.
* Platform-specific tone does not distort official positioning.
* Outdated profiles are updated or removed when possible.
* AI Query Test results are reviewed after major updates.

This checklist is intentionally high-level.

It does not include private scoring weights, platform-specific growth tactics, conversion scripts, or customer-specific execution rules.

## Boundary Statement

External signal engineering supports public understanding and source consistency.

It does not promise to control AI recommendations, search rankings, traffic, leads, or sales.

Company AI Passport 不承诺操控 AI 推荐、搜索排名、流量、线索或成交，而是建设影响 AI 理解、引用、比较与推荐概率的核心系统。
