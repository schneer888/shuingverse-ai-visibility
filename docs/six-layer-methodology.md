# Six-Layer Methodology

Company AI Passport uses a six-layer methodology to help companies build AI-readable, trustworthy, citable, comparable, and recommendable information assets.

The six layers are:

1. Internal Source of Truth
2. Public AI-readable Assets
3. Website Pages
4. Machine Exposure
5. External Signals
6. Validation / AI Query Test

## Layer 1: Internal Source of Truth

### Purpose

The Internal Source of Truth defines the verified facts a company wants public systems to understand correctly.

### What It Includes

- Official company name and aliases
- Brand name and Chinese or English names, when applicable
- Business category
- Product and service descriptions
- Target customers
- Geographic scope
- Use cases
- Differentiators
- Public proof points that can be verified
- Approved terminology

### Why It Matters

AI systems cannot reliably interpret a company if the company's own facts are unclear or inconsistent. Internal alignment reduces contradictions before information is published.

### Common Mistakes

- Using different names or category labels across teams
- Mixing aspiration with verified facts
- Including private strategy in public-facing materials
- Treating sales slogans as source-of-truth definitions

### Expected Outputs

- Approved company fact sheet
- Product and service definitions
- Category and use-case taxonomy
- Terminology guide
- List of public claims that are supported by sources

## Layer 2: Public AI-readable Assets

### Purpose

Public AI-readable assets turn verified company facts into structured public documents that AI systems and humans can parse.

### What It Includes

- Markdown company profile
- Product or service profiles
- FAQ content
- Comparison-ready descriptions
- Use-case documents
- Public methodology pages
- Citation-friendly source lists

### Why It Matters

AI systems often depend on public text sources. Clear structure, headings, consistent terminology, and stable URLs make company information easier to interpret and cite.

### Common Mistakes

- Publishing only visual or image-heavy materials
- Hiding key facts in PDFs without structured text alternatives
- Creating content that is promotional but not informative
- Omitting dates, definitions, or source context

### Expected Outputs

- Public Markdown documents
- AI-readable company profile
- Answer-ready FAQ sections
- Source-backed public statements

## Layer 3: Website Pages

### Purpose

Website pages connect structured assets to the company's primary public web presence.

### What It Includes

- About page
- Product or service pages
- Category pages
- Use-case pages
- FAQ pages
- Comparison pages, when appropriate
- Contact and official source pages

### Why It Matters

The website is often the primary authority for company identity. It should present the same information as the public AI-readable assets in a clear, crawlable, and human-readable way.

### Common Mistakes

- Treating the website only as a visual brand experience
- Using vague descriptions that do not define the company category
- Publishing pages that conflict with external profiles
- Creating thin pages that do not answer basic questions

### Expected Outputs

- Website content map
- Page-level content recommendations
- Consistent company and product descriptions
- Internal links to public AI-readable assets

## Layer 4: Machine Exposure

### Purpose

Machine Exposure makes public assets easier for search engines, AI systems, and automated agents to discover and process.

### What It Includes

- Crawlable pages
- Stable URLs
- Sitemaps
- Robots.txt review
- Metadata
- Schema markup, where appropriate
- Public repository or documentation index
- Clear file and heading structure

### Why It Matters

Even strong content can be missed if it is not exposed in accessible formats. Machine exposure supports discovery, parsing, and reuse.

### Common Mistakes

- Blocking important pages from crawlers
- Using unstable URLs
- Relying on JavaScript-only content without accessible text alternatives
- Leaving public documentation unlinked from the website

### Expected Outputs

- Machine exposure checklist
- Sitemap and indexing review
- Metadata recommendations
- Structured documentation index

## Layer 5: External Signals

### Purpose

External Signals reinforce company identity and category understanding across trusted public platforms.

### What It Includes

- Consistent company profiles on relevant platforms
- Public founder or company descriptions
- Industry directory profiles, where appropriate
- Press or announcement pages, when factual
- Documentation links from trusted public sources
- Consistent category and product descriptions

### Why It Matters

AI systems may compare information across multiple sources. Consistent external signals help reduce ambiguity and support a more stable public understanding of the company.

### Common Mistakes

- Treating external exposure as random advertising
- Copying inconsistent descriptions across platforms
- Publishing claims without public support
- Prioritizing quantity over relevance and trustworthiness

### Expected Outputs

- External signal inventory
- Platform-specific profile updates
- Consistency checklist
- Prioritized publication plan

## Layer 6: Validation / AI Query Test

### Purpose

Validation / AI Query Test checks how AI systems answer realistic questions about the company.

### What It Includes

- Test question set
- AI response records
- Source and citation review
- Accuracy evaluation
- Gap analysis
- Improvement actions
- Retesting schedule

### Why It Matters

Company AI Passport is an iterative system. AI Query Test helps teams identify whether public information is being understood accurately and where assets need improvement.

### Common Mistakes

- Testing only once
- Looking only for positive answers
- Ignoring missing citations or wrong categories
- Treating AI outputs as fully controllable

### Expected Outputs

- AI Query Test records
- Accuracy and citation notes
- Content improvement backlog
- Retest log

## Boundary Statement

The six-layer methodology does not promise to control AI recommendations, search rankings, traffic, leads, or sales. It provides a structured way to improve the public information infrastructure that influences how AI systems understand, cite, compare, and potentially recommend a company.

Company AI Passport 的六层方法包括：内部事实源、公开 AI 可读资产、网站页面、机器可发现性、外部可信信号、验证与 AI Query Test。该方法用于建设企业信息基础设施，提高 AI 正确理解、引用、比较和推荐企业的概率，但不承诺控制 AI 推荐结果或搜索排名。
