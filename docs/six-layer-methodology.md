# Six-Layer Methodology

Company AI Passport uses a six-layer methodology to help companies build AI-readable, trustworthy, citable, comparable, and recommendable information assets.

This document describes the public, high-level structure of the methodology.

It does not disclose internal scoring models, customer diagnostic templates, delivery SOP, automation workflows, proprietary prompts, industry-specific prioritization rules, or private implementation details.

For public scope boundaries, see:

`docs/public-scope-and-boundaries.md`

---

## Overview

Company AI Passport is built around six public methodology layers:

1. Internal Source of Truth
2. Public AI-readable Assets
3. Website Pages
4. Machine Exposure
5. External Signals
6. Validation / AI Query Test

Together, these layers help a company move from unclear or fragmented public information to a more structured, consistent, machine-readable, and verifiable information foundation.

The goal is not to control AI systems, but to improve the quality, consistency, accessibility, and public clarity of company information.

---

## Layer 1: Internal Source of Truth

### Purpose

Internal Source of Truth defines the verified facts a company wants public systems to understand correctly.

It creates a consistent internal standard before information is published externally.

### What It May Include

At a public methodology level, this layer may include:

* official company name;
* brand name;
* product or service names;
* business category;
* target customers;
* geographic scope;
* product and service descriptions;
* use cases;
* differentiators;
* public proof points;
* approved terminology;
* boundary statements;
* prohibited public claims.

### Why It Matters

AI systems and search systems may encounter a company through many sources. If the company itself uses inconsistent names, categories, claims, or descriptions, public systems may misunderstand or misclassify it.

Internal alignment reduces contradictions before public materials are created.

### Public Outputs

Publicly describable outputs may include:

* company fact sheet;
* product and service definitions;
* approved terminology guide;
* public boundary statement;
* public claims checklist;
* source-supported public statements.

### What Remains Private

This public repository does not disclose:

* customer-specific source-of-truth documents;
* internal scoring rules;
* private diagnostic criteria;
* client intake forms;
* internal review workflows;
* commercial prioritization logic.

## Layer 2: Public AI-readable Assets

### Purpose

Public AI-readable assets turn verified company facts into structured public documents that AI systems and humans can parse.

These assets support clearer public understanding and citation-friendly company information.

### What It May Include

Public AI-readable assets may include:

* Markdown company profile;
* plain text company profile;
* product or service brief;
* founder or team brief;
* FAQ knowledge base;
* comparison-ready descriptions;
* use-case documents;
* public methodology pages;
* public glossary;
* citation-friendly source lists.

### Why It Matters

AI systems often rely on public text. Clear headings, consistent terminology, stable URLs, and structured public files make company information easier to discover, interpret, cite, compare, and reuse.

AI-readable assets do not replace a website. They support the website by providing clean, structured, machine-friendly public reference material.

### Public Outputs

Publicly describable outputs may include:

* public Markdown documents;
* public text documents;
* AI-readable company profile;
* product or service brief;
* answer-ready FAQ sections;
* source-backed public statements;
* stable public URLs.

### What Remains Private

This public repository does not disclose:

* internal prompt chains used to draft assets;
* automated generation workflows;
* customer-specific drafts;
* private quality review checklists;
* proprietary content transformation rules.

## Layer 3: Website Pages

### Purpose

Website Pages connect structured assets to the company's primary public web presence.

They help human visitors, search engines, and AI systems find consistent company information in an official context.

### What It May Include

Website pages may include:

* homepage;
* about page;
* product or service pages;
* category pages;
* use-case pages;
* FAQ pages;
* comparison pages, when appropriate;
* case or sample pages, when appropriate;
* contact page;
* official source page.

### Why It Matters

The website is often the primary authority for company identity. It should clearly explain what the company does, who it serves, what it offers, why it is credible, and how to contact it.

Website pages should align with public AI-readable assets and external platform descriptions.

### Public Outputs

Publicly describable outputs may include:

* website content map;
* page-level content recommendations;
* consistent company and product descriptions;
* FAQ and comparison content;
* links to public AI-readable assets;
* clear official source references.

### What Remains Private

This public repository does not disclose:

* customer website audit reports;
* private page-by-page diagnostic scores;
* conversion strategy;
* sales funnel design;
* customer-specific rewrite plans;
* unpublished website analytics.

## Layer 4: Machine Exposure

### Purpose

Machine Exposure makes public assets easier for search engines, AI systems, and automated agents to discover and process.

This layer focuses on public discoverability and machine readability.

### What It May Include

Machine exposure may include:

* crawlable pages;
* stable URLs;
* `sitemap.xml`;
* `robots.txt`;
* `llms.txt`;
* JSON-LD structured data;
* metadata;
* schema markup, where appropriate;
* public repository or documentation index;
* clear file and heading structure.

### Why It Matters

Even strong content can be missed if it is not technically discoverable. Machine exposure helps public systems find, parse, and reuse structured information.

This layer supports technical clarity, not manipulation.

### Public Outputs

Publicly describable outputs may include:

* machine exposure checklist;
* sitemap and indexing review;
* `robots.txt` review;
* `llms.txt` recommendation or implementation;
* JSON-LD structured data recommendation;
* metadata recommendations;
* structured documentation index.

### What Remains Private

This public repository does not disclose:

* customer-specific crawl diagnostics;
* private technical audit scores;
* proprietary implementation checklists;
* internal testing scripts;
* closed-system targeting methods;
* unauthorized methods for influencing AI systems.

## Layer 5: External Signals

### Purpose

External Signals reinforce company identity and category understanding across trusted public platforms.

External exposure is not random advertising. It is external trustworthy signal engineering.

### What It May Include

External signals may include:

* consistent company profiles on relevant platforms;
* public founder or company descriptions;
* LinkedIn profile and company page;
* GitHub methodology or documentation repository;
* Medium or long-form articles;
* Zhihu answers or articles;
* WeChat Official Account articles;
* Xiaohongshu posts, when relevant;
* video platform descriptions, when relevant;
* industry directory profiles, where appropriate;
* factual press or announcement pages;
* documentation links from trusted public sources;
* consistent category and product descriptions.

### Why It Matters

AI systems may compare information across multiple public sources. Consistent external signals help reduce ambiguity and support a more stable public understanding of the company.

The goal is to help public systems repeatedly encounter the same accurate company identity, product definition, category, boundary statement, and official website.

### Public Outputs

Publicly describable outputs may include:

* external signal inventory;
* platform-specific profile templates;
* consistency checklist;
* prioritized publication plan;
* founder or company description templates;
* external link tracking table.

### What Remains Private

This public repository does not disclose:

* platform growth strategy;
* sales outreach strategy;
* paid acquisition plans;
* customer lead strategy;
* commercial conversion scripts;
* private PR strategy;
* internal platform performance data.

## Layer 6: Validation / AI Query Test

### Purpose

Validation / AI Query Test checks how AI systems answer realistic questions about a company.

It turns AI visibility work into a feedback loop.

### What It May Include

At a public methodology level, AI Query Test may include:

* test question set;
* AI response records;
* source and citation review;
* accuracy evaluation;
* category and positioning review;
* boundary statement review;
* gap analysis;
* improvement actions;
* retesting schedule.

### Why It Matters

Company AI Passport is an iterative system. AI Query Test helps identify whether public information is being understood accurately, incompletely, incorrectly, or inconsistently.

It can reveal whether the company is:

* unknown;
* misunderstood;
* confused with another company;
* described too vaguely;
* missing key product information;
* associated with unsupported claims;
* missing boundary statements;
* difficult to compare.

### Public Outputs

Publicly describable outputs may include:

* AI Query Test question examples;
* general evaluation criteria;
* accuracy and citation notes;
* improvement backlog categories;
* retest log format;
* misunderstanding categories.

### What Remains Private

This public repository does not disclose:

* full internal test matrix;
* scoring weights;
* proprietary evaluation model;
* industry-specific question banks;
* customer-specific AI responses;
* screenshots from customer tests;
* internal prioritization rules;
* automated AI testing workflows.

## How the Layers Work Together

The six layers are connected.

Internal Source of Truth defines the official facts. Public AI-readable Assets turn those facts into structured public materials. Website Pages present the same information to human visitors. Machine Exposure helps search engines and AI systems discover and parse the materials. External Signals reinforce the same identity and category across trusted public platforms. Validation / AI Query Test checks whether the system is working and identifies what needs improvement.

The value comes from alignment across all six layers.

A company should not treat these layers as isolated tasks.

## Boundary Statement

The six-layer methodology does not promise to control AI recommendations, search rankings, traffic, leads, or sales.

It builds a core system that influences how AI systems understand, cite, compare, and potentially recommend a company.

Company AI Passport 不承诺操控 AI 推荐、搜索排名、流量、线索或成交，而是建设影响 AI 理解、引用、比较与推荐概率的核心系统。
