# Comparison: SEO, GEO, AEO, and Company AI Passport

Company AI Passport is related to SEO, GEO, and AEO, but it is broader in scope.

It treats company information as a public infrastructure system for the AI search and agent recommendation era.

## Overview

SEO, GEO, and AEO each address important parts of discoverability and answerability. Company AI Passport connects these concerns with internal truth, public AI-readable assets, website pages, machine exposure, external signals, and validation.

## SEO

SEO stands for search engine optimization.

It generally focuses on improving how web pages are discovered, indexed, and ranked by search engines.

Common SEO concerns include:

- Technical crawlability
- Page structure
- Keywords and search intent
- Internal linking
- Backlinks
- Page performance
- Search snippets

## GEO

GEO is often used to refer to generative engine optimization.

It generally focuses on how generative AI systems may retrieve, interpret, synthesize, and cite public information.

Common GEO concerns include:

- Source clarity
- Structured content
- Citation-friendly pages
- Entity understanding
- Context completeness
- Consistency across sources

## AEO

AEO stands for answer engine optimization.

It generally focuses on making content easier for answer engines to use in direct responses.

Common AEO concerns include:

- FAQ structure
- Concise answers
- Definitions
- How-to content
- Question-and-answer formatting
- Featured-answer readiness

## Company AI Passport

Company AI Passport is a standardized company information system.

It helps companies build AI-readable, trustworthy, citable, comparable, and recommendable information assets through a six-layer methodology:

1. Internal Source of Truth
2. Public AI-readable Assets
3. Website Pages
4. Machine Exposure
5. External Signals
6. Validation / AI Query Test

## Comparison Table

| Area | Primary Focus | Typical Unit | Main Question | Company AI Passport Relationship |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| SEO | Search discoverability | Web page | Can search engines discover and rank the page? | Uses SEO-compatible structure but is not limited to ranking. |
| GEO | Generative AI interpretation | Public source or content set | Can generative systems understand and cite the information? | Includes GEO concerns within a broader company information system. |
| AEO | Direct answers | Question and answer | Can an answer engine respond clearly from this content? | Uses answer-ready assets as part of public AI-readable documentation. |
| Company AI Passport | Company information infrastructure | Company profile and asset system | Can AI systems understand, cite, compare, and potentially recommend the company based on public information? | Provides the full methodology across internal truth, assets, website, machine exposure, external signals, and validation. |

## Why Company AI Passport Is Broader

Company AI Passport is broader because it starts before publication and continues after publication.

It includes:

- Internal fact alignment
- Structured public assets
- Website page consistency
- Machine exposure
- External trustworthy signal engineering
- AI Query Test validation
- Iterative improvement

It is not only about optimizing a page, a query, or a single answer. It is about building a reliable company information foundation.

## What It Does Not Replace

Company AI Passport does not replace:

- Product strategy
- Brand strategy
- Website design
- SEO execution
- Public relations
- Sales operations
- Customer success
- Legal or compliance review

Instead, it provides a structured information layer that these functions can reference and align with.

## Boundary Statement

Company AI Passport does not promise to control AI recommendations, search rankings, traffic, leads, or sales. It builds the foundational infrastructure that influences how AI systems understand, cite, compare, and potentially recommend a company.
